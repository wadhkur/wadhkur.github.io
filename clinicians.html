For referring clinicians

Collaboration that respects your clinical plan

Referral intake is structured, reporting is concise, and communication remains direct with the physician overseeing the imaging.

Referral essentials

  • Clinical questionClear indication helps align protocol and measurements.
  • Prior studiesPrevious imaging informs comparison and avoids duplication.
  • Patient considerationsAllergies, devices, and tolerance factors noted up front.

Workflow overview

Request

Send the clinical question and preferred timeframe. Urgency is accommodated when possible.

Protocoling

Protocol is selected and confirmed, including contrast considerations and sedation needs if relevant.

Reporting

Reports highlight key findings and actionable impressions. Supplemental measurements are available on request.

Communication preferences

Direct physician-to-physician discussion is welcomed for complex cases or evolving questions.

If you prefer structured templates, indicate your requirements and they will be used in reporting.

Turnaround expectations

Scheduling and reporting timelines are aligned with the requested urgency. Please flag time-sensitive studies at referral.

  • Interim updatesPreliminary impressions can be shared when clinically useful.
  • Comparative reviewPrior studies are incorporated when available.
